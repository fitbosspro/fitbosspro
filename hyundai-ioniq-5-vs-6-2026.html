SUV vs. Sedan: Two Takes on Excellence

The Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Ioniq 6 share the same award-winning E-GMP platform, 800V architecture, and cutting-edge technology. But they serve different masters. The Ioniq 5 is a spacious, retro-styled compact SUV. The Ioniq 6 is a sleek, ultra-aerodynamic sedan with record-breaking range. Here's how to choose between them.

The Ioniq 5 starts at approximately $35,000, making it slightly more affordable than the Ioniq 6's $37,500 entry point . Both offer exceptional value, but the Ioniq 5 includes more standard utility features, while the Ioniq 6's higher price reflects its aerodynamic design and longer range potential .

‚ö° Range & Efficiency: Ioniq 6 Is the King

The Ioniq 6 84 kWh RWD achieves up to 680 km (423 miles) on the WLTP cycle, making it one of the longest-range EVs on the market . The Ioniq 5 maxes out at 318 miles EPA (about 512 km) . The Ioniq 6's ultra-low 0.21 drag coefficient gives it a clear efficiency advantage‚Äîideal for road-trippers.

üîå Charging: Identical Excellence

Both vehicles share Hyundai's 800V E-GMP architecture, enabling 350 kW DC fast charging and 10-80% charges in just 18 minutes . For 2026, both models now feature NACS ports, granting direct access to Tesla's Supercharger network‚Äîa game-changer for road trips.

ü™ë Interior & Practicality: Ioniq 5 for Families

The Ioniq 5 offers 26.3 cubic feet of cargo space behind the rear seats, expanding to 58.5 cu ft with seats folded . Its sliding rear seats and flat floor make it incredibly versatile. The Ioniq 6, as a sedan, offers about 15.8 cu ft of trunk space (including a small frunk) . For hauling gear, kids, or bikes, the Ioniq 5 is the obvious choice.

üöó Driving Dynamics: Different Personalities

The Ioniq 5 is comfortable and composed, but steering feel is "vague" . The Ioniq 6, with its lower center of gravity and sedan shape, is more engaging to drive. The new Ioniq 6 N, with 641 hp and a 3.2-second 0-60 time, is a genuine sports sedan rivaling the BMW M3 . The Ioniq 5 N is also thrilling, but it's a heavier, SUV-shaped hot hatch.

Our Take
