Two Electric SUVs, Two Very Different Missions

The Tesla Model Y and Kia EV9 are two of the most compelling electric SUVs in 2026. But they target different buyers. The Model Y is a sleek, efficient, two-row crossover with Supercharger access. The EV9 is a boxy, three-row family hauler with genuine adult space in the third row. Here's how they compare.

The Tesla Model Y starts at $44,990, making it significantly more affordable than the EV9's $54,900 entry point . The EV9's higher price reflects its larger size, third row, and 10-year warranty .

‚ö° Range & Efficiency: Tesla Still Wins

The Model Y is the efficiency champion, with the Long Range RWD achieving 391 miles WLTP (approx. 337 miles EPA) . The EV9's Light Long Range RWD offers 305 miles EPA, which is respectable but trails the Tesla by a significant margin . In real-world highway driving, the Model Y will consistently go farther on a charge.

The EV9 also retains its 800V architecture, enabling 10-80% charges in 24 minutes vs. the Model Y's ~25 minutes . Charging speeds are comparable, but the EV9's 800V system can maintain higher speeds longer .

This is the clearest differentiator. The EV9 offers genuine three-row seating with 30.8 inches of legroom in the third row‚Äîenough for adults on shorter trips . The Model Y's optional third row was always tight and is discontinued for 2026 . Cargo space heavily favors the EV9: 20.2 cubic feet behind the third row vs. the Model Y's 12.8 cubic feet behind its second row .

üöó Driving Dynamics: Different Personalities

The Model Y, especially with the 2026 "Juniper" updates, is more engaging to drive. It feels lighter, more responsive, and corners with confidence . The EV9 is larger and heavier, but still drives well‚ÄîKelley Blue Book notes it "glides around town" with "good visibility" and "a quiet cabin" . It's not sporty, but it's serene and comfortable.

üìã Quick Verdict: Which Should You Buy?

Choose the 2026 Tesla Model Y if:

  • You don't need a third row (or have small children who fit in the discontinued 7-seat option)
  • You prioritize efficiency and maximum range
  • You want the most engaging driving dynamics
  • You value the proven Supercharger network (though the EV9 now has access too)
  • You're on a tighter budget‚Äîthe Model Y is significantly cheaper

Choose the 2026 Kia EV9 if:

  • You need genuine three-row seating for adults or older children
  • You want maximum cargo space and family-friendly practicality
  • You value Kia's 10-year warranty and long-term peace of mind
  • You prefer physical controls and a more traditional interior layout
  • You want 800V ultra-fast charging (comparable to Tesla)
Our Take

These SUVs serve different masters. The Model Y is the efficient, engaging choice for individuals or small families. The EV9 is the spacious, practical choice for larger families who need three rows. With the EV9 now having NACS Supercharger access, the charging network advantage has narrowed considerably. Test-drive both‚Äîthe size difference alone will make your decision clear.
