What's the difference between a hybrid and a plug-in hybrid?
A standard hybrid charges its small battery via regenerative braking and the gas engine. A plug-in hybrid has a larger battery that can be charged from an outlet, giving you 20-40 miles of all-electric driving before the gas engine kicks in .
How long does it take to charge a PHEV?
On a Level 2 (240V) charger, most PHEVs charge in 2-4 hours. On a standard household outlet (Level 1), expect 5-8 hours for a full charge .
Do plug-in hybrids qualify for tax credits?
Yes‚Äîmany PHEVs still qualify for up to $7,500 federal tax credit, depending on battery size and assembly location . The Prius Prime, RAV4 Prime, and BMW X5 PHEV are examples of eligible models.
What PHEV has the longest electric range?
The Toyota Prius Prime leads with 44 miles of EV range, followed closely by the RAV4 Prime at 42 miles .
Is a PHEV worth it if I can't charge at home?
You'll still get hybrid fuel economy (40-50 MPG) without charging, but you won't realize the full EV benefits. If you can't charge, a standard hybrid might be a better value .
