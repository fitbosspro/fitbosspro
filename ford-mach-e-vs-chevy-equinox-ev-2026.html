Ford Mach-E vs Chevy Equinox EV 2026 Comparison

February 18, 2026 By Cynthia Ebony 13 min read
Two compelling electric SUVs: Ford's sporty Mach-E and Chevy's value-focused Equinox EV
Two Electric SUVs, Two Different Personalities

You're ready to go electric, and you've narrowed it down to two of America's most compelling options: the Ford Mustang Mach-E and the Chevrolet Equinox EV. Both are excellent, but they approach the electric SUV formula from completely different angles. The Mach-E leans into performance and Mustang heritage. The Equinox EV prioritizes value, range, and everyday practicality. Here's how they compare.

Pricing and Value

The Chevrolet Equinox EV starts at $34,995, making it significantly more affordable than the Ford Mustang Mach-E's $42,840 base price . That's a difference of nearly $8,000. The Equinox EV also qualifies for the federal tax credit, further narrowing the gap for eligible buyers . The Mach-E's price climbs quickly as you add options—the GT trim with California Special package pushes past $55,000 .

Practical tip: If budget is your primary concern, the Equinox EV is the clear winner. The base LT model delivers 319 miles of range for thousands less than the Mach-E's entry point .

Range and Efficiency

Spec Mach-E Equinox EV
Base Range 240 miles 319 miles
Max Range 320 miles 319 miles
AWD Range 240-280 miles 307 miles

The Equinox EV leads in base range by a substantial margin—319 miles for the front-wheel-drive model versus 240 miles for the base Mach-E . The Mach-E closes the gap with its extended-range trims, reaching up to 320 miles, but those cost significantly more . The Equinox EV delivers 319 miles at an affordable price point, which is its biggest selling point .

Performance and Driving Dynamics

This is where the Mach-E shines. The base Mach-E Select produces 325 horsepower, while the GT trim delivers 480 horsepower and hits 60 mph in just 4.4 seconds . The Equinox EV's front-wheel-drive model is notably slower, taking 7.7 seconds to reach 60 mph . The all-wheel-drive Equinox EV is much more competitive, with 300 horsepower and a 5.7-second 0-60 time .

MotorTrend notes that the Equinox EV's suspension is tuned for a "nice balance of ride quality and handling," but it's not a sporty vehicle . The Mach-E, especially in GT trim, is genuinely engaging, with sharper throttle response and sport-tuned handling .

Charging and Infrastructure

The Mach-E supports DC fast charging up to 170 kW, slightly faster than the Equinox EV's 150 kW maximum . In real-world terms, the Equinox EV takes about 48 minutes for a 5-80 percent charge, adding 81 miles of range in 15 minutes . The Mach-E's faster peak rate gives it a slight edge on road trips.

Both vehicles offer access to extensive charging networks. The Mach-E uses Ford's BlueOval network, while the Equinox EV relies on public networks and home charging . For 2026, both are compatible with the North American Charging Standard (NACS) via an adapter, granting access to Tesla Superchargers .

Interior and Cargo Space

The Equinox EV features a massive 17.7-inch infotainment screen and an 11-inch digital gauge cluster . However, it notably lacks Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, relying instead on Google built-in apps . The Mach-E offers a 15.5-inch vertical touchscreen with Ford's Sync system, including wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto .

Cargo space is similar, with the Mach-E offering 59.5 cubic feet with seats folded versus the Equinox EV's 57.2 cubic feet . The Mach-E also features a unique washable frunk for additional storage .

Safety and Driver Assistance

Both SUVs come standard with comprehensive safety suites. The Equinox EV includes Chevy Safety Assist with forward collision alert, automatic emergency braking, lane keep assist, and blind spot monitoring . Higher trims offer GM's excellent Super Cruise hands-free driving system .

The Mach-E features Ford Co-Pilot360 with similar standard features, and available BlueCruise hands-free driving on mapped highways . Both vehicles have earned top safety ratings from NHTSA and IIHS .

Expert Ratings

Car and Driver rates the 2026 Chevrolet Equinox EV 8.5 out of 10, calling it "a strong contender that finally brings electric practicality to the Chevy showroom" . MotorTrend praises its "competitive pricing" and "commendable range" . The Ford Mustang Mach-E earns strong reviews as well, with reviewers highlighting its performance credentials and engaging driving dynamics .

Quick Verdict: Which One to Buy

Choose the 2026 Ford Mustang Mach-E if:

  • You prioritize performance and driving engagement
  • You want the 480 hp GT trim with sporty handling
  • You prefer Mustang-inspired styling and road presence
  • You need wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto
  • You're willing to pay a premium for power and style

Choose the 2026 Chevrolet Equinox EV if:

  • You want maximum range for your dollar (319 miles)
  • You're budget-conscious—the Equinox starts $8k lower
  • You prefer a more traditional SUV shape and interior
  • You value GM's Super Cruise hands-free system
  • You don't need Apple CarPlay (Google built-in works for you)
Our Take

The Equinox EV is the smarter choice for most buyers. It delivers exceptional range at a breakthrough price, wrapped in a practical, family-friendly package. The Mach-E is the enthusiast's choice—more expensive, less efficient, but genuinely fun to drive. As MotorTrend noted, the Equinox EV "feels like a better deal than a Model Y—if you can live without the badge" .

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has more range?
The Chevrolet Equinox EV offers up to 319 miles of range with front-wheel drive. The Ford Mustang Mach-E achieves up to 320 miles with extended-range RWD trims, but those cost significantly more .
Which is faster?
The Ford Mustang Mach-E GT produces 480 horsepower and hits 60 mph in 4.4 seconds. The Equinox EV AWD manages 5.7 seconds, while the FWD model is slower at 7.7 seconds .
Does the Equinox EV have Apple CarPlay?
No, the Chevrolet Equinox EV does not offer Apple CarPlay or Android Auto. It uses Google built-in apps like Google Maps and Google Assistant instead .
What's the price difference?
The 2026 Chevrolet Equinox EV starts at $34,995, while the Ford Mustang Mach-E starts at $42,840—a difference of nearly $8,000 .
Do both have Super Cruise/BlueCruise?
Yes. The Equinox EV offers GM's Super Cruise hands-free driving on higher trims. The Mach-E offers Ford's BlueCruise system for hands-free highway driving .
